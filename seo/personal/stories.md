Trying something completely new at 67 when the odds are stacked against you and going all in - is beyond just inspiring.

That’s the story of an elderly couple from Junnar. They started growing grapes in a region where no one attempts it.

The reason I could relate so much to it is - my parents have been tradition grape growers. One thing I know for a fact is - It’s extremely hard even for seasoned, experienced growers.

The sheer amount of variables you have to deal with are just too may. The real challenge though is - you control less than 70% of them..

I remember - We once had finalized the deal and the harvesting was supposed to start next day. That night there was hailstorm and it was 100 to 0 over night.

So - a huge shout out to this brave couple. It was not easy for them - took them 5 years to see the light. After 2 straight bad years - they did not switch back to what they were growing traditionally.

LI POST 4:

I started working at QuestionPro right out of college, in fact - while I was in college. That was way back in 2007! I remember seeing a news paper ad for night shift support job! 

I was struggling to pay for my college, and tried all kinds of random part time jobs that had nothing to do with tech - so thought this would be a good opportunity. That's how it all started. 

Since then, it has been way more than a roller coaster ride! I was at QuestionPro for 18 years! That's almost half my life. Not a big fan of counting years - as years will pass independent of what you do. 

Early days, I played different roles like support, QA, account management, Customer Success (that term was probably not there at that time). But then quickly moved to Engineering -  and no it was not that i had a calling etc. It was just that whatever be the role  - I was ready for it! 

Turned out Engineering was my calling. I was heading the Engineering at QuestionPro. From a single head to heading & growing - was a long journey! 

End of 2024, we were a team of 70 (Engineering). 

It was all amazing, compensation, empowerment, autonomy - you name it! It was almost like running your own Enterprise...

You guessed it right - I quit in December. 

Why? 
Better offer - Hell.. NO.
Better work life balance - NO. We used to sleep in the office first few years ;)

I had to answer this question to myself before I took the decision. 

1. It was getting too predictable for me personally. I read somewhere today - Its almost like playing a fixed match. No one wants to play a fixed match, in fact no one wants to watch a fixed match! It's the uncertainties that make things exciting. 

2. The other thing was - I wanted to do something I had never done before, give it a shot in true sense! Stress testing yourself. I am deeply optimistic about that. 

3. You have one life! 

How did I finally came to the decision?

Letting it all go. Status quo. It's going to be ok with - 
- 1 less car upgrade. 
- 1 less house upgrade. 
- 1 less foreign travel. 
- Fewer Friday night outings. 

Believe me. It's easier said than done, when you have lived it the other way for so long and the social influence we live in. But then as they say if - If you live by people's acceptance, you die by their rejection. 

Quit and do what?
Well.. Build an AI powered tool, driven by AI, Insights by AI.. ;)

I have been building a next gen customer success platform - that never forgets to follow up on QBR ;)

Sales - I have only sold to Engineers in the past. So, selling software is my first huddle to glory! 

It's been 3 months without a pay check. There are moments of moments of doubt and despair - But, then if it was not for that, all this would have been pointless ;) 

I know its hard - and I might fall.. but what if I Fly!
